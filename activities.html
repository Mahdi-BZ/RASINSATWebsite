National Robotics Weekend (NRW) is a three-day congress that takes place during the first three days of spring break, dedicated to IEEE student members in Tunisia. The congress comes with a very rich and diverse program, allowing students from different levels and different fields of study to learn, test their skills and build a solid network. The idea of the congress came from a need to provide IEEE student members with the skills that will help them in their careers as future engineers. The congress is an opportunity for members to keep up with the latest technologies in relation to robotics and apply their knowledge through concrete projects and contests.

NRW 1.0 included four challenges . An exhibition challenge, a CAD challenge using SolidWorks and an entrepreneurial challenge.

NRW 2.0

Previous Next

The second edition of National Robotics Weekend (NRW) was held on the 17th, 18th and 19th of March 2019, in El Kantaoui Center Hotel, Sousse Governorate in Tunisia. The theme of this year’s edition was “Smart Cities”, which is a new concept that NRW wanted to spread among young engineering students, considering how important it can become in Tunisia.
The program offered diverse workshops delivered by certified instructors during the first two days. These workshops covered both soft skills and technical skills respectively on the first and second day of the congress. Technical skills:
CAD using SolidWorks, Raspberry Pi, Machine learning applied to robotics, Internet of Things (IoT). Soft skills:
NLP ( Neuro-Linguistic Programming ), Business Plan, Digital Marketing, Leadership.
The program also included three challenges, a 24 hour IoT challenge, a CAD challenge and an exhibition challenge.
